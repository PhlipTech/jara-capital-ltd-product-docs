1. Purpose and audience

This PRD translates the Business Requirements Document into functional behaviour, user flows, data ownership, and platform split:

Surface Users Role
Mobile app (iOS / Android) Consumers (B2C) Sign-up, profile, eKYC via PhlipVerify (liveness, BVN, NIN, address, etc.), Cowrywise linkage, wallet, products, invest, portfolio, withdraw; phased: Auto-Save, lending / overdraft when API and compliance allow
Web admin Internal ops, support, compliance (roles TBD) View users, linkage/KYC status (read-heavy), audit, configuration (non-secret), support actions

Never in-house (any phase): Custody, fund management, core banking rails as your own regulated stack; crypto unless explicitly added.

MVP core: Savings and investment flows via Cowrywise Embed (wallet, VA, catalog, invest, portfolio, withdraw as API supports).

Phased products (in this PRD): Auto-Save (rules, mandates or partner recurring debits, round-ups if offered) and lending / overdraft (eligibility, limit, drawdown, repayment, collateral tied to savings per BRD). Delivery depends on Cowrywise Embed (or another licensed partner) exposing credit and recurring-debit capabilities; until then, requirements below define UX, audit, and integration hooks.

2. Product summary

2.1 Vision

Enable Nigerians to save and invest through a single mobile experience while Cowrywise provides regulated accounts, wallets, products, and settlement via the Embed API.

2.2 Value proposition

  • Users: Simple flows: register → eKYC via PhlipVerify → link Cowrywise account → fund wallet → choose product → track performance → withdraw/redeem where supported.
  • Business: Fast time-to-market; compliance and custody delegated to Cowrywise; app owns UX, acquisition, and engagement.
  • Partner: Cowrywise supplies API; app must honour terms, rate limits, and server-side-only credentials.

3. Assumptions and constraints

3.1 Assumptions

  • Cowrywise Embed API exposes: accounts, wallets (incl. virtual account), products, fund investment from wallet, transactions, portfolio/positions, withdrawals/redemptions as documented at developers.cowrywise.com. Identity and eKYC are not performed by Cowrywise in this architecture.
  • PhlipVerify is the identity provider for liveness, BVN, NIN, address, and other checks you enable; all PhlipVerify calls are server-side from your backend; the app stores verification outcomes, references, and timestamps (NDPR-aligned minimisation).
  • Primary currency NGN; USD (or other) products only if exposed by API.
  • Users can fund via bank transfer to virtual account (NIP/transfer).
  • Auto-Save may use partner recurring instructions, bank mandates, or in-app scheduled wallet transfers, subject to NDPR consent and partner API.
  • Lending / overdraft is originated and serviced by a licensed partner; the app surfaces limits, draws, schedules, and statements via API; credit decisions and ledger of record stay with the partner.

3.2 Constraints

  • No Cowrywise secrets in mobile clients. All Embed API calls from backend; mobile talks to our API only.
  • NDPR: lawful basis, privacy notice, data minimisation, retention policy, user rights (access/erasure where applicable).
  • AML/KYC: Identity verification is satisfied via PhlipVerify outcomes; Cowrywise may still enforce its own account rules before wallet or invest. App surfaces combined status and blocks invest/withdraw per policy when PhlipVerify or Cowrywise indicates not ready.
  • Rate limits: implement backoff, queues for async jobs, user-visible retry messaging.

5. Mobile app – information architecture (MVP)

Suggested navigation (tab or drawer):

  1. Home / Dashboard – wallet balance, portfolio summary, quick actions (Add money, Invest).
  2. Invest – product catalog, filters, product detail, invest flow.
  3. Portfolio – positions, performance summary, drill-down.
  4. Activity – transaction list and filters.
  5. Auto-Save (phased) – rules, next run, history of debits, pause/cancel.
  6. Borrow / Overdraft (phased) – eligibility, limit, drawdown, schedule, repayments; separate UX for term loan vs revolving overdraft.
  7. Profile – personal details, PhlipVerify eKYC status (liveness, BVN, NIN, address as enabled), bank accounts (if required by withdraw), mandates/consent for Auto-Save and auto-repay, legal (terms, privacy), support, logout.

Deep links (future): Post-KYC return URL, email magic links (if used).

8. Data model overview (backend)

8.1 Principles

  • Source of truth for money and positions: Cowrywise. Source of truth for identity checks: PhlipVerify outcomes as stored in your DB (references and statuses). App DB holds references, cached read models for UX, and consent/audit.
  • PII: Minimise; align retention with NDPR and policy.
  • Idempotency: Store idempotency keys for invest/withdraw requests to safely retry.

11. Product offerings reference (marketing / catalog copy)

The BRD appendix describes Target / Fixed / Flex savings, mutual funds, fixed notes, T-Bills, real estate, dollar products, Auto-Save (round-ups, salary %, scheduled debits), Quick loans (e.g. up to a share of savings), and Overdraft (revolving line tied to history and average balance).

MVP catalog is whatever Cowrywise Embed exposes today; marketing and in-app labels must match live API products and regulatory disclosures.

Auto-Save and lending / overdraft are in this PRD as phased capabilities (sections 4.9–4.10, mobile IA, flowcharts, ERD extensions). They ship when the partner exposes recurring debits, credit limits, drawdown, and repayment APIs (or another licensed credit partner is integrated). Cards and unrelated forex remain out unless explicitly added.
